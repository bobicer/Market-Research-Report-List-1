<p><p>Polydioxanone suture, also known as PDO suture, is a synthetic absorbable suture material commonly used in various surgical procedures. Several factors contribute to this market growth. Additionally, advancements in surgical techniques and an increasing focus on patient recovery and comfort are boosting the adoption of PDO sutures due to their biocompatibility and reduced tissue reactivity.</p><p>Moreover, the market for polydioxanone sutures is benefiting from the growing geriatric population, as elderly individuals often require surgical interventions. <p><p>Polydioxanone (PDO) suture is a type of absorbable surgical suture used in various medical procedures. It is available in two market types: Monofilament and Multifilament. Monofilament sutures consist of a single filament, providing smooth passage through tissues and reducing the risk of tissue trauma and infection. Multifilament sutures, on the other hand, are composed of multiple filaments braided together, offering increased flexibility and handling characteristics. Both types serve the purpose of wound closure, but each offers distinct advantages in terms of tissue interaction and surgeon preference.</p></p>
