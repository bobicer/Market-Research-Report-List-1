<p><p>A floor-mounted fan coil is an HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning) unit that provides heating and cooling by forcing air over a coil that is either heated or cooled. These fan coil units are typically installed on the floor, either vertically or horizontally, and are connected to a central heating or cooling system.</p><p>The floor-mounted fan coil market is witnessing significant growth due to various factors. Firstly, the increasing demand for energy-efficient HVAC systems is driving the market. Floor-mounted fan coils consume less energy compared to other HVAC systems, making them more environmentally friendly and cost-effective for consumers. Additionally, the growing construction industry, especially in emerging economies, is boosting the demand for floor-mounted fan coils.</p><p>Moreover, the rising popularity of smart homes and smart buildings is propelling the market growth. Floor-mounted fan coil units can be integrated with smart home systems, allowing users to control the temperature remotely. This convenience factor has led to increased adoption of these units.</p><p>Furthermore, technological advancements in fan coil units, such as the development of quieter and more efficient fans, are positively impacting the market. Manufacturers are also focusing on improving the design and aesthetics of these units to cater to the growing demand for visually appealing HVAC systems.</p><p>According to the market growth analysis, the floor-mounted fan coil market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 13.1% during the forecast period. <p><strong>The Floor Mounted Fan Coil Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Surface Mounted</li><li>Conceal Install</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The floor-mounted fan coil market consists of two types: surface mounted and concealed install. In surface mounted fan coils, the units are installed visibly on the floor, usually against a wall. These units are easily accessible for maintenance and repairs. On the other hand, concealed install fan coils are hidden within the structure of a building, such as in ceilings or false floors. These units provide a more aesthetically pleasing appearance as they seamlessly blend into the surroundings while maintaining functionality.</p></p>
