<p><p>The Pelton turbine is a type of hydraulic turbine used in the hydropower industry. It was invented by Lester Allan Pelton in the 1870s and is widely recognized for its efficiency and reliability in converting water's potential energy into mechanical energy. The Pelton turbine operates by directing a high-velocity jet of water onto buckets mounted on a wheel, creating a rotational movement that drives the generator to produce electricity.</p><p>The Pelton Turbine Market has witnessed steady growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 6% during the forecast period. This versatility has further contributed to the market's growth.</p><p>Moreover, technological advancements in turbine design and materials are also impacting the Pelton turbine market. <p><p>Pelton Turbines are used for hydroelectric power generation and consist of a rotor with multiple buckets. Single nozzle market type features a single water jet that strikes the rotor, directing its energy into rotational movement. Twin-nozzle market type involves two water jets striking the rotor simultaneously, increasing power output. Multi-nozzle market type incorporates multiple water jets that strike the rotor, further enhancing power generation. These differing market types cater to varying power requirements and efficiency levels, allowing for flexibility in turbine selection based on specific project needs.</p></p>
