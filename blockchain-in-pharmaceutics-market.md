<p><p>In the pharmaceutics market, private blockchain refers to a decentralized ledger system accessible only to a single organization. It allows the company to securely track and verify the supply chain, ensuring transparency and preventing counterfeit drugs. On the other hand, public blockchain is open to everyone, offering complete transparency and immutability, enabling various stakeholders to verify the integrity of pharmaceutical transactions. Lastly, consortium blockchain involves multiple organizations collaborating to operate a shared blockchain, bringing both security and transparency benefits while allowing for efficient inter-organizational transactions in the pharmaceutics market.</p></p>
