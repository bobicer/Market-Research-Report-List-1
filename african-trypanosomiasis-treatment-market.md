<p><p>African Trypanosomiasis, also known as sleeping sickness, is a parasitic disease caused by the Trypanosoma parasite. The disease affects both humans and animals and is transmitted by the tsetse fly found in sub-Saharan Africa. It is characterized by fever, headaches, joint and muscle aches, fatigue, and in later stages, neurological symptoms.</p><p>The treatment of African Trypanosomiasis typically involves the use of medications to kill the parasite and control the symptoms. The two main drugs used for treatment are pentamidine and suramin for the first stage of the disease, while melarsoprol and eflornithine are used for the second stage. In recent years, there has been a shift towards using eflornithine as the first-line treatment due to its lower toxicity compared to melarsoprol.</p><p>The African Trypanosomiasis Treatment Market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years.
