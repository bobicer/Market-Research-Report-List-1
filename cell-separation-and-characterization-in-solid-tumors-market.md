<p><p>Cell separation and characterization in solid tumors refer to techniques used to isolate and identify specific types of cells in tumor samples. These techniques include fluorescence-activated cell sorting (FACS), magnet-activated cell sorting (MACS), density gradient centrifugation, dielectrophoresis, aqueous two-phase system, and field flow fractionation. FACS uses fluorescent markers to label and sort cells based on their specific characteristics. MACS relies on magnetic beads to separate target cells from a mixture. Density gradient centrifugation separates cells based on their density. Dielectrophoresis uses electrical forces to isolate cells. Aqueous two-phase system separates cells based on their affinity for different liquid phases. Field flow fractionation separates cells based on their size and other physical properties.</p></p>
