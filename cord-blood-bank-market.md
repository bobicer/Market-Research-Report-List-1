<p><p>Cord blood banks can be divided into two main types: public cord blood banks and private cord blood banks. Public cord blood banks collect and store donated cord blood units from volunteer mothers. These units are made available to the general public and can be used for transplants by anyone in need. In contrast, private cord blood banks collect and store cord blood units exclusively for a specific family's personal use. These units are reserved for the family's own potential future medical needs.</p></p>
