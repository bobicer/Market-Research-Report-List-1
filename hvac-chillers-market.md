<p><p>HVAC chillers are central cooling systems used to cool large commercial, industrial, and institutional buildings. They work by removing heat from a fluid (usually water or a mixture of water and glycol) and transferring it to the air through a refrigeration process. The chilled water is then distributed to various areas of the building for air conditioning or process cooling purposes.</p><p>The HVAC chillers market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue expanding at a CAGR of 9.3% during the forecast period. This enables better control, monitoring, and optimization of the cooling process, leading to improved energy efficiency and sustainability. Moreover, the integration of digital controls and advanced sensors allows for predictive maintenance, reducing downtime and maintenance costs.</p><p>Another trend is the growing adoption of modular chiller systems, which offer flexibility in terms of capacity and easier installation, allowing for more efficient space utilization. Additionally, there is an increasing focus on eco-friendly and low-global warming potential (GWP) chillers, driven by stringent environmental regulations and the need to reduce carbon emissions.</p><p>In conclusion, the HVAC chillers market is projected to observe steady growth in the coming years, driven by the increasing demand for energy-efficient and sustainable cooling solutions. <p><p>HVAC chillers refer to devices used in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning systems to cool the air or water used for climate control. There are three main types of HVAC chillers in the market. Screw chillers use a rotary screw compressor to cool the refrigerant. Scroll chillers use scroll compressors, which provide higher efficiency and quieter operation. Centrifugal chillers use centrifugal compressors to cool the refrigerant. These chillers differ in their compressor technology, offering various performance levels and cost considerations for different HVAC applications.</p></p>
