<p><p>MRI compatible pacemakers are medical devices that are specifically designed to be safe and compatible for use during magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) procedures. Traditional pacemakers contain metal components that can interfere with the magnetic field generated by MRI machines, potentially causing harm to the patient or damaging the pacemaker itself. MRI compatible pacemakers are made from non-ferromagnetic materials and are designed to withstand the electromagnetic field and radiofrequency pulses produced by MRI machines.</p><p>The global MRI compatible pacemakers market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years due to several factors. Firstly, there is an increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases and a growing number of patients requiring pacemaker implantation. This has led to a higher demand for MRI compatible pacemakers, as patients with these devices often need MRI scans for diagnostic purposes. Additionally, advancements in technology have resulted in the development of more sophisticated MRI compatible pacemakers, which offer improved safety and performance.</p><p>Moreover, the market is also benefiting from the increasing adoption of MRI machines in healthcare facilities worldwide. MRI machines are versatile imaging tools that are used in the diagnosis of various medical conditions, and the demand for MRI scans is growing. As a result, there is a parallel need for MRI compatible pacemakers to ensure that patients with implanted devices can undergo MRI scans safely.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is expected to witness sustained growth in the coming years due to favorable reimbursement policies for MRI scans and technological advancements in MRI imaging techniques. The provision of reimbursement for MRI scans encourages healthcare providers to invest in MRI machines, which in turn boosts the demand for MRI compatible pacemakers.</p><p>Overall, the MRI compatible pacemakers market is expected to continue its growth trajectory, with a projected CAGR of 6.90% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by factors such as the increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, growing adoption of MRI machines, and advancements in technology.</p></p>

<p><p>MRI compatible pacemakers are medical devices that are specifically designed to be used in patients who require a pacemaker and also need to undergo magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) scans. There are three main types of MRI compatible pacemakers available in the market. Single-chamber pacemakers have one lead that is placed in either the right atrium or ventricle. Dual-chamber pacemakers have two leads, one in the right atrium and another in the right ventricle. Biventricular/CRT pacemakers, also known as cardiac resynchronization therapy pacemakers, have three leads that are placed in the right atrium, right ventricle, and left ventricle.</p></p>
