<p><p>The market for Notebook Wireless Network Cards can be classified into two types: Built-In and External. Built-In wireless network cards are integrated within the notebook itself, allowing for easy portability and convenience. They are usually smaller in size and provide a seamless connection. On the other hand, External wireless network cards are separate devices that can be connected to the notebook via a USB or PCI slot. They offer the advantage of being easily upgradeable and provide better range and performance options.</p></p>
