<p><p>A Germanium Wafer (Ge Wafer) is a thin slice of germanium crystal used in electronic devices. The market for Ge wafers is categorized into two types: Conductive Type P-type and Conductive Type N-type. P-type wafers have impurities that create holes or positively-charged carriers, allowing the flow of positive current. N-type wafers are doped with impurities that produce negatively-charged carriers or electrons, enabling the flow of negative current. These different types cater to specific electronic applications and allow for the creation of various electronic devices.</p></p>
