<p><p>Fluid therapy is a medical treatment that involves the administration of various fluids to patients in order to correct imbalances or maintain hydration. The market for fluid therapy includes different types: colloid, crystal liquid, and blood. Colloid fluids contain particles suspended in a liquid, such as albumin or gelatin, which help increase blood volume and stabilize blood pressure. Crystal liquids are clear solutions containing electrolytes, often used for hydration and to restore fluid balance. The blood market involves the transfusion of whole blood or blood components to replace blood loss or treat specific conditions.</p></p>
