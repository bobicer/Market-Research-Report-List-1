<p><strong>The Chemical Sensors Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Electrochemical Sensors</li><li>Optical Sensors</li><li>Semiconductor Sensors</li><li>Pellistor/Catalytic Bead Sensors</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Chemical sensors are devices used to detect and measure the presence of specific chemical substances in a given environment. Electrochemical sensors are based on chemical reactions that occur at an electrode, producing an electrical signal proportional to the concentration of the target substance. Optical sensors utilize light absorption or emission to identify chemicals. Semiconductor sensors rely on changes in electrical conductivity when exposed to target gases. Pellistor/Catalytic bead sensors measure combustible gases based on their ability to catalyze combustion. Lastly, other types of sensors employ different principles like gas chromatography or mass spectrometry for chemical detection.</p></p>
