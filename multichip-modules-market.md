<p><p>Multichip Modules (MCMs) are advanced integrated circuits that combine multiple chips in a single package to enhance performance and reduce size. The laminated type MCMs consist of multiple chips sandwiched and interconnected by multiple layers of insulating materials. Stacked MCMs involve vertically stacking chips and bonding them with through-silicon vias. Other types of MCMs include system-in-package (SiP) and ceramic MCMs. SiP integrates various components like chips, passive components, and interconnects in a single package, while ceramic MCMs use ceramic substrates for enhanced thermal performance. These MCM types cater to different application needs, offering flexibility and efficiency in electronic designs.</p></p>
