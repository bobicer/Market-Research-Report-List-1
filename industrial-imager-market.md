Spectrum Imagers utilize spectral imaging techniques to capture and analyze electromagnetic radiation across a wide range of wavelengths, enabling the identification of materials and the detection of hidden substances. Acoustic Imagers use sound waves to create images by analyzing their reflections, making them ideal for non-destructive testing and inspecting the integrity of solid structures. <p><p>The industrial imager market finds application in both contact measurement and non-contact measurement processes. In contact measurement, industrial imagers are used to directly obtain data by physically touching the object or surface being measured. On the other hand, in non-contact measurement, industrial imagers capture data without any physical contact with the object or surface, using technologies such as infrared or laser scanning. Both contact and non-contact measurement methods are widely employed across industries for quality control, inspection, and other applications where accurate and efficient measurement is crucial.</p></p>
