<p><p>Property management refers to the overseeing and administration of real estate properties on behalf of the property owner. It involves various tasks such as property maintenance, rent collection, tenant screening, lease enforcement, and financial management. Property management companies act as intermediaries between property owners and tenants, ensuring the smooth operation and profitability of the property.</p><p>The property management market has witnessed significant growth in recent years due to several factors. Firstly, the increasing demand for rental properties and the growing number of real estate investors have driven the need for professional property management services. Additionally, the rising complexity of property laws and regulations has made it challenging for property owners to manage their properties independently, augmenting the demand for property management companies.</p><p>Another key driver of market growth is the adoption of property management software that streamlines various property management tasks, increases operational efficiency, and enhances customer experience. Technology advancements, such as the Internet of Things (IoT), have also contributed to the growth of the market by enabling remote monitoring and control of various property aspects like security and energy management.</p><p>Furthermore, the COVID-19 pandemic has emphasized the importance of property management as property owners sought assistance in dealing with rent collection challenges, maintenance issues, and ensuring compliance with safety protocols.</p><p>According to market analysis, the property management market is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 10.00% during the forecast period. This growth is attributed to the increasing investments in real estate, the expansion of rental markets, and the rising demand for efficient property management solutions. Moreover, the incorporation of advanced technologies such as artificial intelligence and machine learning in property management systems is expected to fuel market growth and provide opportunities for market expansion in the coming years.</p></p>

<p><p>Property management market types can be categorized into two main types: on-premise type and cloud-based type. On-premise property management systems involve the installation and maintenance of software on the user's local server or computer network. This allows greater control and customization but can be more expensive and require technical expertise. On the other hand, cloud-based property management systems are hosted on remote servers and accessible through the internet. They offer flexibility, scalability, and cost-efficiency as updates and maintenance are handled by the provider.</p></p>
