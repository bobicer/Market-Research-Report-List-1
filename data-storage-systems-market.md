<p><ul><li>Direct Attached Storage (DAS)</li><li>Network Attached Storage (NAS)</li><li>Storage Area Network (SAN)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Data storage systems market types include Direct Attached Storage (DAS), Network Attached Storage (NAS), and Storage Area Network (SAN). DAS involves connecting storage directly to a single computer, providing high-speed access but limited scalability. NAS enables multiple devices to access storage over a network, offering better scalability and simpler management. SAN, on the other hand, utilizes a dedicated network to connect storage devices to servers, optimizing performance, scalability, and flexibility. While DAS is suitable for smaller environments, NAS and SAN cater to larger-scale enterprises with higher demands for storage access and management.</p></p>
