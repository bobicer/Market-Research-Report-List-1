<p><p>The neurointerventional robotic market is categorized into two types: portable and fixed. Portable neurointerventional robots are designed to be mobile and easily transported between different healthcare settings. They offer flexibility and accessibility, allowing healthcare providers to perform procedures in various locations. On the other hand, fixed neurointerventional robots are stationary systems that are permanently installed in specific healthcare facilities. They are optimized for high-performance procedures and offer advanced capabilities but lack the mobility of portable robots. Both types aim to enhance the precision and efficiency of neurointerventional procedures.</p></p>
