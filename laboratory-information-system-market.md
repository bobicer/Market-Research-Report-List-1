<p><p>Laboratory Information System (LIS) is a software system used to manage and track laboratory data. The market for LIS can be divided into two types: standalone and integrated. </p><p>A standalone LIS is a system that operates independently and does not integrate with other healthcare systems. It is typically used in smaller laboratories with limited connectivity requirements. </p><p>In contrast, an integrated LIS is a system that seamlessly integrates with other healthcare systems, such as electronic health records (EHRs) and radiology information systems (RIS). This enables efficient data exchange and collaboration between different departments within a healthcare organization. Integrated LISs are commonly used in larger, multi-departmental laboratories.</p></p>
