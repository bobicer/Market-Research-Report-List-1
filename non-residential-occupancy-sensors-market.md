<p><p>Non-residential occupancy sensors are used to detect the presence or absence of people in non-residential buildings for energy-saving purposes. PIR sensors work by detecting the changes in infrared radiation emitted by human bodies. Ultrasonic sensors use sound waves to sense occupancy, while dual-technology sensors combine both PIR and ultrasonic technologies for more accurate detection. Other types of occupancy sensors include microwave sensors, which emit microwave signals to detect movement, and image sensors, which use cameras to capture images and identify occupancy. These sensors help optimize energy usage by controlling lighting, heating, ventilation, and air conditioning systems based on occupancy levels.</p></p>
