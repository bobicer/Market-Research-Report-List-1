<p><p>Baked cereals are breakfast cereals that are processed through baking instead of the traditional method of extrusion. They are made by mixing various grains, such as corn, wheat, oats, or rice, with ingredients like sugar, salt, honey, and flavors, before being baked to form crispy and crunchy flakes or clusters.</p><p>The baked cereals market has witnessed significant growth in recent years, mainly driven by the increasing preference for healthier and convenient breakfast options. Baked cereals offer several advantages over traditional extruded cereals, such as higher nutritional value, lower fat content, and fewer artificial additives. Consumers are becoming more health-conscious and are opting for products that provide a nutritious start to their day.</p><p>Furthermore, the market growth can also be attributed to the rising demand for gluten-free and organic baked cereals. With the increasing prevalence of gluten intolerance and the growing awareness of the harmful effects of synthetic chemicals, consumers are actively seeking gluten-free and organic alternatives. Baked cereals tailored to these specific dietary needs are gaining popularity in the market.</p><p>Additionally, the hectic lifestyle of modern consumers has fueled the demand for on-the-go breakfast options, making baked cereals an ideal choice. These products offer convenience and ease of consumption, making them suitable for busy individuals or those who prefer quick breakfast options.</p><p>According to market research, the global baked cereals market is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 13.2% during the forecast period. Manufacturers and key players in this market are expected to focus on product innovation and development to cater to evolving consumer preferences and to maintain a competitive edge in the market.</p></p>
