<p><p>Programmable Oscillators are electronic devices used to generate repetitive waveforms or signals. There are two main types: Through Hole Programmable Oscillators and Surface Mount Programmable Oscillators. Through Hole Programmable Oscillators are mounted by inserting their pins into holes on a circuit board, providing a more secure connection and ease of repair. On the other hand, Surface Mount Programmable Oscillators are mounted directly on the surface of a circuit board, allowing for smaller size, increased functionality, and higher precision. Both types offer programmability, allowing users to adjust frequency, voltage, and other parameters.</p></p>
