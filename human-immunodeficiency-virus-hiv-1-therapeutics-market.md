<p><ul><li>Nucleoside-Analog Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors (NRTIs)</li><li>Non-Nucleoside Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors (NNRTIs)</li><li>Entry and Fusion Inhibitors</li><li>Protease Inhibitors (PIs)</li><li>Integrase Inhibitors</li><li>Coreceptor Antagonists</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV)-1 therapeutics market consists of different types of drugs to manage the virus. Nucleoside-Analog Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors (NRTIs) block the enzyme responsible for viral replication. Non-Nucleoside Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors (NNRTIs) inhibit viral replication by directly binding to the reverse transcriptase enzyme. Entry and Fusion Inhibitors prevent the virus from entering and fusing with host cells. Protease Inhibitors (PIs) block the viral enzyme protease, preventing the production of infectious viral particles. Integrase Inhibitors block the viral enzyme integrase, hindering the integration of viral DNA into host cells. Coreceptor Antagonists inhibit viral entry by blocking specific chemokine receptors on host cells.</p></p>
