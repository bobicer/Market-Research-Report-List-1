<p><p>Air-electrode batteries are a type of rechargeable battery that use atmospheric oxygen as the cathode material. Zinc-air batteries utilize zinc as the anode and oxygen from the air as the cathode, offering a high energy density and long shelf life. On the other hand, lithium-air batteries use a lithium anode and oxygen as the cathode, providing an even higher energy density. Both types have the advantage of lightweight and high energy storage capacity, making them potential candidates for various applications such as electric vehicles and portable electronics.</p></p>
