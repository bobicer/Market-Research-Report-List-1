<p><p>Dental electric motors are divided into two types: high-speed and low-speed motors. High-speed motors are designed for tasks requiring fast rotation, such as cutting through hard materials like enamel or bone. They offer higher torque and speed, facilitating efficient procedures. On the other hand, low-speed motors are used for more delicate tasks, like polishing or grinding, requiring slower and controlled rotations. These motors typically have a smaller size and offer greater precision, allowing dentists to perform intricate procedures with accuracy and finesse.</p></p>
